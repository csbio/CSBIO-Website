Dr. Nelson successfully defended his dissertation entitled "Genomic Approaches for Characterizing How Chemicals Act on Cells."
He was enrolled in the Biomedical Informatics and Computational Biology program (BICB) and has accepted a position at Yumanity Therapeutics in Boston.
